You are invited to participate in a scientific study. Before proceeding, please consider the following information:

In this study, you will be shown a detailed description of a procedure that was completed by other Prolific participants as part of an experiment. You will be asked to estimate how long each part of the procedure took those participants, on average, to complete.

Participation is voluntary. You may withdraw at any time without penalty.

About the experiment

In a recent experiment on Prolific, participants completed a fictional online government application. Your task is to estimate how long it took them.

What participants did: They role-played as a fictional resident of "Newland" and completed an online application for a Municipal Green Zone Vehicle Access Permit — a permit required to drive in restricted low-emission zones.

The application required them to provide personal details, assess their eligibility, enter vehicle information from official documents, and submit supporting documentation.

One more estimation

You have just estimated how long the procedure took. Now, we would like you to think about the quality of the applications that participants submitted.

⚠️ Application quality estimate
When completing the procedure, participants had to read official documents (driving licence, vehicle registration, insurance certificate, etc.) and transfer the correct information into the application form. They also had to determine eligibility based on a set of rules, and select the right supporting documents to upload.

A quality error means the participant submitted an application containing at least one substantive mistake — information that is wrong or missing compared to what the official documents say. For example:
  • Copying the wrong date of birth from the driving licence
  • Deciding the applicant is not eligible when they actually are (or vice versa)
  • Forgetting to include a required document (e.g., not uploading the technical inspection report when it was needed)
  • Selecting the wrong vehicle fuel type or environmental class from the registration certificate
  • Choosing an expired proof of residence (e.g., a bill that is too old)
These are the kind of errors that would lead to the application being rejected by the administration.

Note: we are not asking about minor formatting issues (like a typo that was immediately corrected by the form). We are asking about substantive mistakes in the final submitted application — errors that the applicant may not have even noticed.
